♂️ Male Reproductive System
🏠 Overview & Location
▼
Testes Ducts Glands Penis
  • Located mostly outside abdomen in the scrotum
  • Scrotum maintains temp 2–2.5°C lower than body → essential for spermatogenesis
🔵 Testes — Structure
▼
🧅 Outer Covering
  • Enclosed by Tunica albuginea
  • ~250 testicular lobules inside
  • Each lobule: 1–3 seminiferous tubules
🔬 Inside Tubules
  • Spermatogenic cells → produce sperms
  • Sertoli cells → nutrition + inhibin
  • Leydig (Interstitial) cells → testosterone
⭐ Memory: Sertoli → Support (nutrition + inhibin) | Leydig → tEstosterone
🛣️ Sperm Route (Duct System)
▼
Seminiferous Tubules →Rete Testis →Vasa Efferentia →Epididymis →Vas Deferens →Ejaculatory Duct →Urethra
📌 Key Stops
  • Rete testis — network of tubules inside testis
  • Vasa efferentia — transport sperm out of testis
  • Epididymis — highly coiled; site of sperm maturation
🔍 Epididymis Parts
Caput (Head) Corpus (Body) Cauda (Storage)
  • Cauda = storage of mature sperm
💧 Accessory Glands
▼
🫙 Seminal Vesicles
  • Secrete fructose + prostaglandins
  • Contribute 60–70% of semen volume
  • Fructose = energy for sperm
🫁 Prostate Gland
  • Milky fluid + enzymes
  • Helps sperm motility & activation
💧 Bulbourethral (Cowper's)
  • Viscous mucus
  • Lubricates urethra
  • Neutralizes acidic traces of urine
🧪 Seminal Plasma
  • = Secretions of ALL 3 glands
  • Semen = Sperm + Seminal Plasma
🎯 Exam Tip: Seminal vesicles = fructose | Prostate = enzymes | Cowper's = mucus
🔵 Structure of Sperm
▼
Head
Nucleus + Acrosome (enzymes for egg penetration)
Neck
Contains Centrioles
Middle Piece
Mitochondria → ATP for movement
Tail
Flagellum for movement / motility

🧫 Gametogenesis
Definition: Formation of haploid gametes (sperm + ovum) from diploid germ cells. Includes Spermatogenesis, Spermiogenesis & Oogenesis.
🔵 Spermatogenesis — Hormonal Control
▼
GnRH (Hypothalamus) → Anterior Pituitary → LH → Leydig cells → Testosterone
                                 → FSH → Sertoli cells → spermatogenesis + inhibin
📊 Stages of Spermatogenesis
▼
1
Multiplication Phase (Mitosis)
  • Spermatogonia (2n) divide repeatedly
  • Some remain as reserve stem cells, some → Primary Spermatocytes
2
Growth Phase
  • Primary spermatocyte (2n) grows in size
  • Largest cell in the lineage
3
Meiotic Divisions
  • Meiosis I: Primary spermatocyte (2n) → 2 Secondary spermatocytes (n)
  • Meiosis II: Each → 2 Spermatids (n)
  • Result: 1 primary spermatocyte → 4 spermatids
⭐ Spermiogenesis: Spermatids (non-motile) → Spermatozoa (motile) — includes tail, acrosome, nuclear condensation, mitochondrial spiral
⭐ Spermiation: Release of mature sperm from Sertoli cells into lumen of seminiferous tubules
🌸 Oogenesis
▼
Begins before birth, pauses, resumes at puberty
🤰
Before Birth
  • Oogonia (2n) → Primary oocytes (2n) by mitosis
  • Primary oocytes start Meiosis I → STOP at Prophase I
  • Surrounded by follicle cells → Primordial follicle
  • At birth: ~1–2 million oocytes
🌸
At Puberty
  • ~60,000–80,000 oocytes remain
  • Monthly: Primordial → Primary → Secondary → Tertiary → Graafian follicle
⚡
Before Ovulation (Meiosis I Completes)
  • Primary oocyte (2n) → Secondary oocyte (n) + First polar body (n)
  • Secondary oocyte starts Meiosis II → STOPS at Metaphase II
🎯
After Fertilization (Meiosis II Completes)
  • Secondary oocyte → Ovum (n) + Second polar body
  • If no fertilization → Meiosis II never completes

🩸 Menstrual Cycle
Definition: Cyclic, rhythmic changes in female reproductive system every ~28 days involving ovary, uterus, and hormones. Occurs from Menarche → Menopause.
🩸 Phase 1 — Menstrual Phase (Day 1–5)
▼
  • Corpus luteum degenerates → Progesterone drops
  • Endometrium breaks down → discharged as menstrual flow
  • Blood loss: 30–80 mL
  • FSH starts rising again toward end of phase
🌱 Phase 2 — Follicular/Proliferative Phase (Day 6–14)
▼
  • FSH ↑ → stimulates follicular growth
  • Growing follicles secrete estrogen
  • Estrogen → proliferation (thickening) of endometrium + vascularization + gland development
  • Follicle: Primary → Secondary → Tertiary → Graafian follicle
Peak estrogen → positive feedback → sudden LH surge → triggers ovulation
🍒 Day 14 — Ovulation
▼
  • Triggered by sharp LH surge
  • Graafian follicle ruptures
  • Releases secondary oocyte (arrested at Metaphase II)
  • Picked up by fimbriae of fallopian tube
🌼 Phase 3 — Luteal/Secretory Phase (Day 15–28)
▼
  • LH converts ruptured follicle → Corpus luteum
  • Corpus luteum secretes progesterone (high) + some estrogen
  • Progesterone → endometrium becomes thick, glandular, vascular → ready for implantation
❌ No Fertilization
  • Corpus luteum → Corpus albicans
  • Progesterone falls
  • Endometrium degenerates → menstruation
✅ Fertilization Occurs
  • Corpus luteum maintained by hCG (from embryo)
  • Pregnancy begins
  • Menstruation stops

🔬 Fertilization & Implantation
Site: Ampullary–isthmic junction of fallopian tube | Definition: Fusion of sperm + ovum → zygote
⚡ Pre-Fertilization Steps
▼
1. Capacitation
  • Biochemical changes in sperm in female tract
  • Removes glycoprotein coat on sperm head
  • Makes sperm membrane fusible
2. Acrosomal Reaction
  • Acrosome releases enzymes: Hyaluronidase + Acrosin
  • Penetrates corona radiata + zona pellucida
3. Species-Specific Recognition
  • Zona pellucida has ZP3 receptor
  • Binds only to human sperm
🛡️ Blocks to Polyspermy
▼
Zona Reaction
Zona pellucida hardens immediately after sperm entry
Cortical Reaction
Cortical granules release enzymes → prevent entry of additional sperms
🧬 Zygote → Blastocyst
▼
Male pronucleus (n) + Female pronucleus (n) → Zygote (2n) → 2-cell → 4-cell → 8-cell → Morula (~16 cells, solid) → Blastocyst
Morula reaches uterus 3–4 days after fertilization
Trophoblast (outer)
→ forms placenta
ICM (inner)
→ forms embryo
Blastocoel
Fluid-filled cavity inside blastocyst
🌷 Implantation
▼
Definition: Attachment of blastocyst to endometrium | Occurs ~6–7 days after fertilization
1
Apposition
Blastocyst loosely contacts endometrium
2
Adhesion
Trophoblast cells tightly bind to endometrium
3
Invasion
  • Trophoblast → Cytotrophoblast (inner) + Syncytiotrophoblast (outer)
  • Syncytiotrophoblast erodes uterine tissues → blastocyst embeds
  • Starts producing hCG
🤰 Pregnancy, Parturition & Lactation
Pregnancy: Implantation → Parturition, ~9 months / 40 weeks. Maintained by hCG → Corpus luteum → Progesterone
🫙 Placenta — Structure & Functions
▼
  • Temporary, disc-shaped foetal–maternal organ
  • Formed by: Chorionic villi (foetal) + Uterine tissue (maternal)
Transport
Nutrients (glucose, amino acids), O₂ in, CO₂ + urea out
Barrier
Protects from many pathogens (NOT all)
Endocrine Gland
Secretes multiple hormones (see below)
Placental Hormones
HormoneFunction
hCGMaintains corpus luteum early pregnancy; basis of pregnancy test
hPL (human placental lactogen)Breast development, maternal metabolism
RelaxinRelaxes pelvic ligaments, dilates cervix
Progesterone & EstrogenMaintain endometrium, suppress ovulation

👶 Parturition (Childbirth)
▼
Trigger: Foetal ejection reflex → Hypothalamus → Oxytocin (posterior pituitary) → uterine contractions
🔄 Positive Feedback: Oxytocin → contractions → stretch cervix → more oxytocin → birth
3 Stages of Parturition
1. Dilation
Cervix dilates
2. Expulsion
Baby delivered
3. Afterbirth
Placenta expelled
🍼 Lactation
▼
Prolactin
Anterior pituitary → milk production
Oxytocin
Posterior pituitary → milk ejection (let-down reflex)
🌟 Colostrum = first yellowish milk | Rich in IgA antibodies | Highly nutritious | Passive immunity
  • Suckling → ↑ Prolactin (produce milk) + ↑ Oxytocin (release milk)
  • Benefits: passive immunity, ideal nutrition, emotional bonding
